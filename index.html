Problem Statement:

Affinity Diagram:

This Affinity Diagram is breaking down our Problem Statement "Students may arrive at the food court for lunch only to find that the lines are too long for them to get lunch before their next class." We have the subcatagories technology, user experience, administrative changes, and business. Under technology, we have features of the application that would need to be developed. Under user experience, we have methods that we can develop to help the user. Under business, we have future developments that can be made in order to move into different fields. Under administrative changes we have problems that may come up in the future.

Team Sketches

Sketch 1:
The first screen shows the main screen listing different locations. For each location, there is a logo, name, distance, wait time, and a visual aid of the business. On the top of the screen there is the current searching location and average business level in the area. On the bottom there are options for different pages like favorites, map, main, search, and profile.
The second screen shows the map page of the application. In it there is a heat map of each location that gives the user an approximation of business level.
The third screen shows the search page of the application. It has a search bar at the top for specifc searches and a slection of food by type that the use can choose from.

Sketch 2:
The first screen is the login page of the application. Centered is a login button. On the bottom is the menu where you can select search, home, or map.
The second screen is the home screen. On the top is the location and the search radius. The user can change both of these. The main focus of the home screen is the food list. It is has a section for closetst to the user location, lowest wait time in the radius, and the favorites the user has previously marked.
The third screen is the search page of the application. It has a search bar for specific searches and filters to further narrow down the results.
On the top right of every page is the profile icon and selection button.

Sketch 3:
The first screen is home screen that shows the application name and partners. On the top right is a sign in button and there is also the opption to scroll to see the locations.
The second screen lists locations in your area that you can view.
The third screen is the page that appears when you select a location from screen 2. This page shows the wait time based on the average and similar orders to the users.
